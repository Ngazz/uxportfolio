Background

M-PESA is a mobile money transaction platform launched in 2008 by a giant Telecomunication Company In Kenya, Safaricom. By the end of 2023, it had over 36million users across Kenya.

Overview

During the COVID19 pandemic, majority of people in Kenya migrated to cashless transactions. Despite M-PESA being a successful moble money transfer platform, its reliance on mobile devices exposes the mobile users to threats of losing such devices to muggers and other criminals.

Problem is that ...

People relying on mobile phones for cashless transactions become a target by criminals and muggers. A report published by The Star Newspaper 12 Dec, 2022 shows that at least one person loses a mobile phone in every 10 Minutes.

Research - Let's find why

We had to investigate the process of mobile money transactions and the overal time taken by muggers to make a way with mobile devices, through observation, interviews and recorded video footages.

Key challenges

In conclusion, we decided to focus on the following areas to design a Wearable financial transaction application ‐ M-PESA Smart

Users have a challenge of carrying out transactions with a fear of losing their mobile devices

Users find it hard to trust other system that does not show realtime balance and previous transactions.

Financial information and balance is a personal affair. Users feel insecure sharing their account balance,

Business goal

Retaining customers on the M-PESA platform and keping them engaged.

Users goal

Providing an efficient and seamless cashless transaction with ease.
